I've always admired people who write consistently. Not because they produce viral content, but because the act of writing forces clarity. You can't explain something you don't understand.

## The goal

I want to use this blog as a thinking tool. When I learn something new — a pattern, a debugging technique, an idea that changes how I see a problem — I want to write it down. Not for an audience, but for my future self.

## Why it's hard

Writing takes effort. It's much easier to consume than to create. Reading a tutorial gives you the illusion of understanding. Writing your own explanation tests whether you actually got it.

The other obstacle is perfectionism. Every draft feels rough, unfinished, not ready. But a published rough post is infinitely more useful than an unpublished perfect one.

## The plan

One post a week, minimum. Short is fine. The point isn't volume — it's the habit of processing thoughts into words.

Let's see how it goes.
